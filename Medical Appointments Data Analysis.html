Introduction¶

The purpose of this research was to investigate why 30% of patients miss their scheduled appointments, and through using the No-show appointments data from Kaggle, I explored important factors from the dataset that influence whether a patient shows up for their scheduled appointment using analysis tools. I plotted four independant variables(age,gender,welfare,neighbourhood) against the dependendant variable(No_show) and divided them into two research quetions, namely:

  • How does age and gender affect patients showing up to their scheduled appointments?
  • How does welfare and neighbourhood impact patients showing up to their scheduled appointments?

The conclusion of these findings can assist in predicting whether a patient will show up for their scheduled appointment.

Step 14: I then created a function to group the Ages and added an extra column(AgeGroup) with the grouped lables.

From ages:

  • 0 - 20 = Child
  • 20 - 40 = Young Adult
  • 40 - 60 = Adult
  • 60 - 80 = Mature Adult
  • 80+ = Advanced Years

The purpose of this was to assist in showing the numericals of each age group of patients who show up and those who don't which will further be represented in a histogram in which the bins were set to 20

In [70]:
def Agerange(Age):
    if Age <= 20:
       return 'Child'
    elif Age <= 40:
       return 'Young Adult'
    elif Age <= 60:
     return 'Adult'
    elif Age <= 80:
     return 'Mature Adult'
    else :
        return 'Advanced Years'

df['AgeGroup']=df['Age'].apply(Agerange)

Conclusions¶

Research Limitations :

  • There was lack of clarity on the dataset description, some information was availbale through Kaggle forum posts
  • No official confrimation of where the data comes from
  • Lack of information on how many health units share from the same database, this may lead to data inconsistancy
  • The lack of data on the neighbourhood variable which doesn't indicate the location of the patient makes it restricting to make an in depth analysis of how impactful the location of the health centre is on the patient

The purpose of this research was to investigate why 30% of patients miss their scheduled appointments, the following independant varibles gave us the folliwing insights:

  • Age

The older a person gets the more the no-show rate decreases The rates are the highest when the patient is young and they decrease significantly when the patients age reaches 60+

The higher rates may occur because: Parents or guardians may have difficulties bringing the child to the appointment And the lower rates may occur because: Older people tend to have more pressing health related issues that must be attended to

  • Gender

There is a significant difference between men and women in the data. One can assume that women generally tend to take better care of their health than men do.

There is a large amount of female patients, in the context of this data, the women and men have a similar no-showing proportion.

  • Welfare

A large number of patients in the dataset don't have welfare. But in comparison to those who do, the patients have similar no show proportions.

  • Neighborhood

The data indicates that that Jardim Camburi is the one with most of the appointments in the state and actually is the most populous neighbourhood from "Esprito Santo".

Since we don't have enough data we can't investigate further for the reasons on why.
